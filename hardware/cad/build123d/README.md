# OSO75 Production Case (build123d)

Parametric B-rep CAD for the OSO75 case, generated by `oso75_case.py` from
the routed PCB (`pcb_extract.json`, dumped from `oso75.kicad_pcb`) and
`hardware/layout/oso75.layout.json`. No other inputs.

Regenerate with `python3 oso75_case.py` (needs `pip install build123d`).
If the PCB ever changes, re-dump `pcb_extract.json` first (see the extract
script in git history / the JSON's provenance).

## Parts

| File | Part | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `oso75_case_bottom.step/.stl` | Bottom tray | filled acoustic deck, screw counterbores, feet recesses |
| `oso75_case_bezel.step/.stl` | Top bezel | clamp step, USB slot, antenna hole, screw pilots |
| `oso75_plate.step/.stl` | Switch plate | 82 MX cutouts, stab + bay openings, 1.5 mm |
| `oso75_bay_cover.step/.stl` | Bay blank | snap-in cover for the empty module bay, with a reduced right lip for keycap clearance |
| `oso75_assembly.step/.glb` | Assembly | all parts in working position |

Outer dimensions: **349.7 x 162.7 x 15.0 mm** (exceeds most consumer FDM
beds — print diagonally on 350 mm+ machines, or CNC/resin/print-farm it).
STEP files open in Fusion/SolidWorks/FreeCAD for fillets, branding, etc.

## Small-bed (A1 mini) segmented variant

For 180 x 180 beds the tray and bezel are also exported as three X-segments
each (`*_seg1..3.stl`, all 83–154 mm wide), joined by glued **stepped lap
joints**: 16 mm overlap, step at mid-height, 0.2 mm glue gap, plus two
vertical 1.75 mm filament alignment-pin holes per seam in the walls. Tray
seams (x = 130/268) and bezel seams (x = 95/205) are staggered so no joint
runs through the assembled case, and every seam is assert-checked clear of
screws, feet, the USB slot and the antenna hole. Glue-up: drop filament
pins in the lower piece's holes, epoxy the lap faces, lower the next
segment on. The laser-cut plate bridges all seams as a stiff spine; the
solid acoustic deck gives each tray seam ~10 cm^2 of glue area.

## Hardware BOM

| Qty | Item | Where |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | M3 x 10 socket head cap screw | from below, through tray into bezel |
| 4 | 10 mm adhesive bumpon feet | recesses in tray underside |
| 1 | RP-SMA bulkhead u.FL pigtail (~100 mm) | back-wall 6.5 mm hole |
| (10) | optional M3 heat-set inserts, OD <= 4.6 | drill bezel pilots to 4.0 first |

Screws self-tap into 2.55 mm pilots — good for FDM/MJF prototypes and small
runs. For injection molding or frequent disassembly, switch to heat-set
inserts (regenerate with `SCREW_PILOT_D = 4.0`).

## Assembly

1. Stick the RP-SMA pigtail's bulkhead in the back-wall half-hole of the
   tray (the hole straddles the parting line, so the cable just drops in).
2. Build the sandwich on the bench: clip all 82 switches into the plate,
   then press the assembly onto the PCB so every switch pin seats in its
   hotswap socket (plate-to-PCB gap = 3.5 mm everywhere). Snap the u.FL end
   onto ANT1 — the cable runs in the 3.5 mm under-plate channel.
3. Lower the sandwich into the tray, USB-C toward the back wall. The PCB
   nests in the lower cavity; the plate's 1.1 mm border rests proud of it.
4. Seat the bezel — its internal step lands on the plate border and the
   USB-C receptacle should be centered in the slot.
5. Flip, drive the 10 M3 screws (snug, do not crank — they clamp the whole
   stack), stick on the feet.
6. Drop the OSO module into the bay opening (top-left). Power-off swaps only.

## Design data (derived, not assumed)

- PCB outline 337.088 x 150.063 from Edge.Cuts; cavity clearance 0.3 mm.
- MX stack: PCB top z7.6, plate top = PCB top + 5.0 (spec), plate 1.5 mm.
- Filled acoustic deck: the under-PCB volume is solid to 0.2 mm below the
  board (no hollow cavity = no drum resonance); only the bottom-side
  keep-outs are pocketed 2.8 mm deep for the 1.85 mm Kailh sockets, solder
  joints, pegs and bottom bodies. Nominal PCB float 0.2 mm (the
  bezel-clamped plate defines stack height).
- USB slot sized to the HRO TYPE-C-31-M-12 body (8.94 x 3.26, +0.4 tol),
  opened down to the parting line; at J1 x = 168.54.
- Antenna hole 6.5 mm for the ESP32-S3-WROOM-1U pigtail, x = 226.
- Stab openings span the actual 3.988/3.048 NPTH pairs on the board +2 mm.
- Module bay opening 54.6 x 25.6 over J3; bezel does not overlap it, so
  modules power-off swap without opening the case. The cover has an asymmetric
  lip plus a keycap-envelope assertion so it does not crowd the adjacent F-row.
- Reset/boot finger holes in the plate above SW_RESET / SW_BOOT.
- Rear plate reliefs clear the USB-C body, ESP32-S3-WROOM-1U module, and
  u.FL pigtail path. Reliefs are assertion-checked against every MX switch
  cutout plus margin so the rear electronics pocket cannot eat F-row plate
  material; still verify the chosen pigtail with a physical coupon.
- Deck pockets are generated from the bottom-side obstacle map extracted
  from KiCad (hotswap socket pads +2.5 mm, every through-hole +1.5 mm,
  bottom-side bodies +1 mm, +0.3 mm margin), plus four spans where the
  stab wires swing below the PCB — so the solid deck only ever touches
  bare soldermask, yet backs every key against typing flex and switch
  hot-swap force.
- Walls 6 mm (house the screw columns), floor 3 mm, corner R4.
- Parting line at z = 7.0; bezel underside sits 0.1 mm above the plate.

## Print/manufacture notes

- Tray and bezel print flat side down, no supports needed except the USB
  slot bridge (9.7 mm — fine) and the antenna half-holes.
- Plate: FDM at 1.5 mm works but flexes; MJF nylon, CNC POM/alu, or laser-cut
  1.5 mm steel/FR4 from `oso75_plate.step` is the production choice.
- Material: PETG/ABS/ASA or MJF PA12 for the case. PLA OK for fit checks.
